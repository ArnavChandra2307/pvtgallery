Regret

I feel like a terrible person sometimes. No matter how hard I try… I mess things up. I try to do good — and somehow, it ends in something worse. It’s like I’m wired to fail, like I’m destined to disappoint. I couldn’t make anyone happy… Not the people I love, not even myself. I keep blaming myself. Not just for what I did, but for what I couldn’t do. For the words I couldn’t say in time, for the moments I didn’t save, for the people I lost without meaning to. And the worst part? I meant well. Always. But good intentions don’t stop people from getting hurt. Sometimes I think — maybe I’m not made for love, or peace, or happiness. Maybe I just break the things I touch. But still, deep down... I want to believe that regret is not the end of my story. That the pain I feel today might build the strength I’ll need tomorrow. I want to believe that I can still grow — even with all the scars. That one day, I’ll look back and be proud that I kept walking, even when I hated every step. For now, I’ll let myself feel it. The ache. The shame. The silence. But I won’t let regret define me forever. Not yet.
